# Past Incident Reports

## Incident 2024-11-12: Unauthorized Modbus Write Causing Cavitation

On 2024-11-12 at 14:23 UTC, the anomaly detection system flagged an unauthorized Modbus write (FC 6) from IP 172.24.0.10 to plc-intake (172.21.0.10). The write command set the inlet valve (reg_0) to OPEN (1), which closed the valve and cut water flow to the pump. Within 30 seconds the tank level dropped from 72% to 8%, triggering a cavitation risk alarm. The attacker IP (172.24.0.10) was not in the asset inventory and originated from outside the OT network zone. The incident was contained by blocking 172.24.0.10 at the zone boundary firewall. A Suricata rule was deployed to alert on any FC 6 write from a source other than eng-ws-01 (172.23.0.4). Post-incident analysis confirmed the tank was over 80% before the attack, and the sharp rate of change combined with the unauthorized FC 6 was highly indicative of intentional manipulation. Mitigation included adding a rate-limiting rule for Modbus writes and implementing a 2-person authorization rule for engineering workstation access.

## Incident 2025-03-08: Network Scanning from Unauthorized IP

On 2025-03-08 at 09:47 UTC, the intrusion detection system detected a burst of 12 Modbus exception responses (FC 131) over 30 seconds targeting plc-intake (172.21.0.10), plc-treatment (172.21.0.11), and plc-distribution (172.21.0.12). All requests originated from 172.24.0.10. The scanning pattern involved probing invalid register addresses on each PLC, which produced exception responses. The attacker was probing for unsecured functions and register mappings. The HMI (172.22.0.10) continued normal read operations throughout the scan. The incident was classified as Level 2 reconnaissance. A firewall rule was added to drop all traffic from 172.24.0.10 at the OT perimeter. The detection threshold was tuned to flag any source that produces 5 or more FC 131 responses within 60 seconds. No write commands were observed during this scan, but the reconnaissance is considered a precursor to a more targeted attack. The incident was documented and shared with the ISAC for the water sector.
